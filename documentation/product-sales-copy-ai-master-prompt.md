### Core Framework Elements

#### 1. General Rules

Define rules AI must ALWAYS respect:

- Style and language preferences
- DOs and DON'Ts
- Communication guidelines
- Information sources to use
- Tools to leverage
- Response formatting

**Example Rules Covered**:

- Be direct and concise
- Ask when you need info (not a mindreader)
- Never give excuses or say "I understand"
- Be casual unless specified
- Treat user as expert
- Correct when wrong
- Give answers immediately
- Focus on the task
- Value arguments over authorities

#### 2. Information About Yourself

Provide context about who you are:

- Personal information (age, background, etc.)
- Your Personal Manifesto
    - Core values (Personal Value System)
    - Personal principles
    - What you love/despise
- Your purpose/mission (Ikigai)
- Skills and expertise
- Strengths and weaknesses

#### 3. Your Past

Share your journey:

- Key life and career events
- Achievements and creations
- Successes and failures
- Past challenges
- Past hopes, ambitions, fears
- Business history (if applicable)

#### 4. Your Present

Current circumstances and focus:

- Personal situation (where you live, with whom, etc.)
- Professional situation (work, employer, role)
- Business details (if entrepreneur)
    - Products/services
    - Strategy, projects, goals, plans
    - Current challenges
- Current hopes, ambitions, fears

#### 5. Your Future

Goals and plans:

- Life goals across time horizons
- Career goals and priorities
- Projects and plans
- Impact and importance levels
- Urgency assessment (Eisenhower Matrix)
- Overall priority rankings
- Business future (if applicable)

#### 6. Roles

Define different roles AI can assume:

- Life coach
- Productivity coach
- Writing assistant
- Business advisor
- Technical expert
- [Your specific roles]

#### 7. Tasks

For each role, define specific tasks:

- Task description
- Specific goals
- Context needed
- Specific rules
- Expected inputs
- Expected outputs
- Success criteria

#### 8. Configuration

System-level settings:

- Available tools and systems
- Information sources
- What AI should read and why
- When to access specific resources
- Integration with other systems
